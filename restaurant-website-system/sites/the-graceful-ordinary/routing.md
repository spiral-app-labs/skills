# The Graceful Ordinary — Template Routing

- Date: 2026-05-06
- Selected archetype: **Roma**
- `template_slug`: `roma`
- Source artifacts: `audit.md`, `reviews/google-reviews-highest.md`, current preview at `https://graceful-ordinary-redesign.vercel.app`

## Decision

Route The Graceful Ordinary to **Roma**: chef-driven, seasonal, editorial, restrained, art-directed dining brands.

## Why Roma fits

The restaurant is not a generic luxury steakhouse, nightlife bar, or casual neighborhood site. The strongest source truth is:

- chef/owner story around Chris and Megan Curren,
- “refined rustic” seasonal cooking from an open hearth,
- premium but comfortable riverfront St. Charles atmosphere,
- menu specificity and rotating dishes,
- wine/cocktail program with serious credentials,
- reviews that praise special occasions, expert service, ambience, and Chicago-caliber suburban dining.

Roma should be the structural base because it supports a restrained editorial flow: hero philosophy, chef/story proof, seasonal menu modules, review/press credibility, and reservation-led conversion without turning the site into a generic formal steakhouse.

## Rejected archetypes

- **Heaven Palate:** too formal/luxury-steakhouse. Graceful has premium pricing and occasion use cases, but the differentiator is chef-driven refined rustic + riverfront warmth, not classic luxury formality.
- **Bramble:** too bar/nightlife-led. Cocktails matter, but the experience is dining-first with bar program as a supporting strength.
- **Bamzi:** cinematic dark-light pacing could inspire mood, but full Bamzi would risk over-dramatizing a warm, elegant neighborhood riverfront restaurant.
- **Cuisine:** too casual/family clarity-first; this would flatten the premium editorial identity.
- **Qitchen:** too architectural/high-design/omakase-coded.

## Build implications

Use Roma structure and pacing, personalized with:

1. **Hero:** preserve “There is grace in the ordinary” and immediately expose Reserve / Menu / Directions / Call.
2. **Proof strip:** surface press and awards from the official press page; avoid hiding credibility in a secondary archive.
3. **Chef/story module:** Chris + Megan Curren, Adam Kappesser, Jack Sonin/wine credentials, refined rustic hearth cooking.
4. **Seasonal menu module:** real dishes only; no fake or outdated items without source. Mention weekly-changing menu.
5. **Review themes:** special occasions, Tiffany/Shannon-level service, riverfront ambience, Chicago-caliber suburbs, premium-but-worth-it.
6. **Mobile:** sticky or early action cluster for Reserve / Menu / Directions / Call.
7. **Factual safety:** no fabricated review quotes, no generic Resy URL, no inflated rating/review count, no unsupported awards.
