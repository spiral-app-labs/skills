# Antojitos Mexicanos La Fonda Pitch Doc

## 60-Second Read

La Fonda already has the hard part: real demand, `4.5` Google stars, `148` reviews, and unusually specific love for Veracruz-style antojitos, tacos, friendly service, and careful takeout. The leak is that Google still shows `Add website`, so a hungry mobile guest has to piece together hours, dishes, proof, phone, directions, and seating details from Google, Restaurantji, Restaurant Guru, and snippets. The prototype gives La Fonda a bright, call-first owned page that concentrates the proof, keeps the Veracruz story front and center, and avoids fake online ordering or fake menu pricing.

## Call Opener

"You already have the reviews and the food people remember — this preview just gives those guests one clean place to call, get directions, and understand why La Fonda is worth the trip."

## What We Fixed

- **Owned conversion home:** Replaced directory hopping with a first-party page that makes phone, directions, hours, and proof immediately visible.
- **Restaurant-specific story:** The page sounds like La Fonda, not a generic taco template: Veracruz-style antojitos, carryout reliability, fresh/fair-value reviews, and named dishes.
- **Truth-safe CTA strategy:** No fake online ordering, full-menu pricing, email, catering, or reservation claims; everything uncertain hands off to a phone call.
- **Mobile sellability:** Added sticky Call/Directions CTAs and fixed responsive rendering so the preview is usable on a phone.
- **Concierge confidence:** Added a helper that answers only from verified facts and refuses to invent prices, seating, allergies, catering, or current specials.

## Demo Path

1. **Open the hero:** Show that the page instantly tells a guest what La Fonda is, where it is, why people trust it, and how to call or get directions.
2. **Scroll to proof + menu highlights:** Point out the public proof strip, tacos/Veracruz cards, and menu language sourced from actual reviews/public listings.
3. **Open the concierge section:** Ask "Can I order online?" or "Do they have dine-in seating?" to prove the site protects the owner by sending uncertain/current details to a phone call.
4. **Tap mobile sticky CTA:** Show that a phone user always has `Call La Fonda` and `Directions` available.

## Do Not Overclaim

- Do not say there is official online ordering; none was verified.
- Do not present prices or a full official menu as final.
- Do not promise dine-in seating; public evidence conflicts, so call to confirm.
- Do not promise catering, allergy handling, delivery providers, reservations, or holiday hours.
- The safest pitch is "this is a first sellable preview built from public proof; owner verification can finalize menu, seating, and provider links."

## Close

"If this already feels more useful than the Google listing, the next step is simply confirming the menu/seating details and turning it into the owner-approved site."
