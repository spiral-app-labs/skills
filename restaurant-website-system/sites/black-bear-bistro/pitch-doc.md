# Black Bear Bistro Pitch Doc

## 60-Second Read

Black Bear already has the hard parts: Chef Santiago and Estela, a creative New American menu, wild game, seafood, vegan and gluten-free options, Square pickup ordering, TableAgent reservations, and a small Main Street room guests describe as a hidden gem.

The leak is that the current site makes the guest work too hard before they can decide: the chef story is underused, the menu is PDF-first, reservations are not the first mobile action, and pickup ordering feels separate.

The prototype preserves the systems that already work and makes the real secret sauce visible: adventurous food, artistic plates, chef care, warm service, and a small room worth booking.

## Call Opener

"You already have the ingredients guests care about - a chef-owned story, adventurous menu, TableAgent proof, Square ordering, and a small room worth booking - so I mocked the version where those strengths are visible in the order diners actually decide."

## Where The Current Site Leaks Revenue

| Leak | Why It Matters | Standard Practice | Prototype Fix |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Menu is PDF-first. | Diners cannot quickly scan wild game, seafood, vegan options, prices, or specials on mobile. | Make the menu readable on-page and keep PDFs as source fallbacks. | Added inline menu sections and menu-first homepage flow. |
| Chef story is underplayed. | The owner-operated care is a trust signal before reviews do any work. | Put the people and kitchen promise near the first decision. | Hero, about, and proof copy foreground Santiago and Estela. |
| Reservation path is not dominant enough. | A small room loses high-intent after-hours guests if booking feels like an errand. | Keep booking CTAs available before and after menu browsing. | TableAgent is surfaced in hero, nav, FAQs, and closeout CTAs. |
| Pickup ordering feels separate. | Square can drive revenue, but only if guests see it at the right moment. | Preserve ordering provider and route to it from menu intent. | Square ordering remains external but is visible beside menu and visit actions. |
| Review proof is not synthesized. | A single quote cannot explain why guests love the place. | Turn repeated themes into a clear proof section. | Review themes now focus on hidden-gem room, chef care, creative menu, sauces, and service. |

## What We Fixed

- **Secret sauce:** The copy now says what guests already say: cozy hidden gem, creative food, chef care, artistic presentation, and special dinners without pretension.
- **Menu path:** Dishes and prices are visible before PDF download, with wild game and vegetarian/gluten-free context easy to scan.
- **Conversion path:** Book table, view menu, order pickup, hours, location, and phone now live in one flow.
- **Owner pride:** The redesign does not make Black Bear fake-luxury; it makes the existing bistro story more legible.

## Demo Path

1. Show the homepage hero and point to the three real actions: Book Table, Our Menu, Order Pickup.
2. Scroll the menu and review section to show creative dishes plus the hidden-gem/chef-care proof.
3. Open the about/contact path to show Santiago and Estela, hours, map, catering/events, Square, and TableAgent preservation.

## Do Not Overclaim

- Verify exact chef-history language before outreach.
- Do not freeze a Google review count without a fresh capture.
- Do not say Square or TableAgent should be replaced.
- Confirm current hours, specials, large-party policy, and which PDF menu is the owner source of truth.

## Close

"This is not a bigger website; it is a clearer front door for the dinner Black Bear already serves."
