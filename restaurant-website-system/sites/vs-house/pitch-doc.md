# V's House — Founder Pitch Doc

## One-line sell story
V's House already has the hard parts: a three-generation Vietnamese family story, a warm designer dining room, a serious pho/sushi/cocktail program, press credibility, and 1,000+ Google reviews. The rebuild turns those assets into an above-the-fold sales story instead of a generic Squarespace welcome page.

## What the current site is costing them
- The homepage leads with “Welcome Home” and `#OneHouseVsHouse`, but does not explain why that promise is true.
- The Vu family history is missing above the fold: Quan Vu opened in 1982, parents Rex and Ann Vu run Pho V in Bedford, and Alex/Ryan/Victoria Vu carry the third generation into V's House.
- The menu is image-based and hard to scan on mobile; it is not searchable, crawlable, or easy to sell from.
- Real proof is buried or absent: Fort Worth Magazine, Dallas Observer, 4.4★ Google / 1,154 reviews, 4.6★ OpenTable, named staff praise, and strong dish-specific demand.
- The existing photo inventory is strong but underused; the site looks template-generic even though the restaurant itself has personality.

## What the new preview fixes
- Repositions the hero around the real hook: “Third Generation on Bedford-Euless Road,” 24-hour pho, Saigon-coded bar, and the Vu family story.
- Uses `bamzi-01` as the locked archetype because V's House is an accessible-casual modern Asian restaurant with menu depth, saturated brand accent potential, and chef/family narrative.
- Promotes the proof stack immediately: family heritage, press, Google/OpenTable review volume, and signature dishes.
- Converts the menu from a pinch-zoom image carousel into structured web content diners can actually browse.
- Preserves real conversion paths: Toast reservations, Toast ordering, phone, directions, gift cards, and social links.

## Demo path for Ethan
1. Start on the old site hero and ask: “Would you know this is a 44-year family food story?”
2. Show the rebuilt hero and trust strip: family since 1982, 24-hour pho, press/review proof.
3. Open the menu section and compare it to the old PNG carousel.
4. Show the bar/cocktail and boba positioning: V's House is more than pho; it is an all-day Vietnamese dining room with a real drink program.
5. End on the reservation/order/contact paths so the owner sees this is not just prettier — it is easier for guests to act.

## Owner-facing pitch angle
“Your restaurant already has the story most places would pay to invent: three generations of the Vu family feeding North Texas since 1982, a beautiful room, a real bar program, and more than a thousand Google reviews. The current site hides that. We rebuilt the site so a guest understands the family, the food, and the reason to book in the first ten seconds — and so the menu works on a phone.”

## Proof to mention
- 4.4★ Google rating with 1,154 reviews from audit evidence.
- 4.6★ OpenTable and 96% Facebook recommend signal from audit evidence.
- Fort Worth Magazine and Dallas Observer coverage referenced in audit.
- Review language around pho, Beef Deluxe Pho, shaken beef, boba, patio, named staff, and warm service.
- Existing official flows: Toast reservations, Toast online ordering, gift cards, phone, directions.

## Risks / honesty notes
- Do not claim a new photoshoot happened; the preview uses the restaurant's existing photo inventory and public business facts.
- Do not over-position as fine dining. The right register is polished accessible-casual Vietnamese / sushi / cocktail bar, not ceremonial tasting menu.
- Google review packet exists as a newest-sorted 30-row sample, not the canonical Highest-filter packet required by the newer agency gate.

## Close
This is a promotion, not a rescue: V's House is already strong in the room, reviews, press, and food program. The preview makes the website finally act like the restaurant is as credible as it really is.
