# El Molino Pitch Doc

## Pitch Position

El Molino does not need a prettier announcement page. It needs a canonical new-chapter site that makes the Zepeda-family story, the renovated room, the menu, the hours, and the guest proof easy to understand in under a minute on a phone.

**One-line pitch:**  
El Molino already has the story: a 1984 Carpentersville landmark, new Zepeda-family management, Puebla-rooted recipes, house salsas, fresh tortillas, margaritas, and a warmer renovated room. The new site makes that story visible and makes it easier for guests to decide, call, visit, and order with confidence.

## Buyer Frame

Speak to the owner/operator as someone trying to protect a legacy restaurant while proving the new management chapter works.

Do not frame this as "your old website is bad." Frame it as:

- The room has changed, but the website has not caught up.
- Guests are already saying the right things; the site should show that.
- The current site is mostly an ownership notice and a PDF menu.
- The new site turns the public story into a clear customer path.

## Current Gaps To Name

1. **The ownership message is defensive.**  
   The current site says new ownership and no direct relation to prior ownership. That is understandable, but it leads with rupture. The better story is stewardship: same landmark, new Zepeda-family chapter.

2. **The menu path is too hard on mobile.**  
   The current PDF forces zooming and panning. The prototype gives guests readable menu highlights with the PDF still available as fallback.

3. **The site does not answer "can I come right now?" fast enough.**  
   The prototype has live open-status behavior, hours, sticky mobile actions, directions, and phone access.

4. **The best proof is hidden.**  
   Guests are noticing the new room, house salsas, fresh tortillas, margaritas, and hospitality. The prototype brings that into a calm horizontal review carousel.

5. **The site does not yet feel like the room.**  
   The prototype uses the renovated-room photography, warm cream/night/mahogany palette, restrained motion, and a host-style AI concierge.

## Prototype Demo Flow

Use this order. Do not wander.

### 1. Open With The Problem

"Right now, the site tells people there is new ownership. That matters, but it does not yet sell the full new chapter. A first-time guest should immediately understand: this is still El Molino, it has a new family team, the room is warmer, and the food guests love is still the reason to come in."

### 2. Show The Hero

Point out:

- El Molino is the first signal, not tiny nav text.
- The new management message is explicit but warmer.
- The photo shows the renovated room.
- Menu and visit actions are available immediately.
- Open status gives live confidence.

Talk track:

"This first viewport is doing more business work. It says who they are, what changed, why the old landmark still matters, and what the guest can do next."

### 3. Show The Proof Strip

Point out:

- 1984 landmark.
- 4.3 public rating from 886 reviews.
- Margarita demand.
- Review audit used as source material.

Talk track:

"These are not decorative stats. They are trust signals that tell a new guest this is a real local place with momentum."

### 4. Show The Story Section

Point out:

- The site reframes new ownership into a family chapter.
- Puebla-rooted recipes and the room both matter.
- Service names like Daniel/Juan become part of the hospitality story without making the page feel like a review dump.

Talk track:

"The current site has the basic fact. This version gives that fact warmth and specificity."

### 5. Show Menu Highlights

Point out:

- Phone-readable sections.
- Signature items.
- PDF is still available, but no longer the only path.
- Pricing clarity can be expanded once the final menu is confirmed.

Talk track:

"This is where we reduce friction. Guests should not have to pinch-zoom a PDF to decide whether to come in tonight."

### 6. Show Eat / Drink / Gather

Point out:

- Eat: tacos, mole, fajitas, tortillas, salsas.
- Drink: margaritas and the renovated bar.
- Gather: birthdays, booths, patio, group dinners.

Talk track:

"This makes the site work for different guest intents: dinner, drinks, and group occasions."

### 7. Show Reviews Carousel

Point out:

- The reviews are presented as social proof, not as loud platform branding.
- No reviewer names cluttering the cards.
- Topics reinforce what guests notice: atmosphere, salsas, date night, service, tortillas, bar.

Talk track:

"This section lets guests hear the room without making the page feel like a review widget."

### 8. Show AI Concierge

Open the "Ask the host" launcher.

Suggested prompts:

- "Are you open right now?"
- "What should I order first?"
- "Can I bring a group?"
- "What changed with new management?"

Talk track:

"This is not a reservation system or an ordering system. It is a host-style guide trained on the restaurant facts, hours, menu highlights, and new-management story. It helps guests get unstuck without forcing them into a giant FAQ page."

Guardrail to say out loud:

"For anything time-sensitive, it points people to call the restaurant."

### 9. Show Visit Section And Sticky Mobile Actions

Point out:

- Address.
- Phone.
- Hours.
- Directions.
- Mobile sticky Menu / Call / Directions.

Talk track:

"The mobile guest never loses the next action."

## What We Are Selling

This is a new customer path for the new chapter:

- Clear first impression.
- Warmer new-management narrative.
- Phone-readable menu.
- Better proof from guest comments.
- Live open-status confidence.
- Directions, phone, and reservation intent.
- AI host for common questions.
- Foundation for SEO and future menu pages.

## What This Is Not

- Not a full online ordering system.
- Not a reservation engine.
- Not final menu/pricing authority until the owner signs off.
- Not a replacement for staff when questions are time-sensitive.
- Not a finished photo library.

## Close

"This prototype is already enough to show the direction. To make it launch-ready, we need owner signoff on wording, final menu/prices, reservation or order links if they want them, the preferred canonical domain, and a small photo pass. The main decision today is whether this is the right story for El Molino's new chapter."

## Owner Asks

Ask for these before launch:

- Confirm final ownership/management wording.
- Confirm canonical domain: `thenewmolino.com` vs any legacy domain path.
- Confirm final menu sections, item names, prices, and add-on/upcharge language.
- Confirm reservation and order links, if any.
- Confirm hours and holiday/closure rules.
- Provide or approve 15-20 photos:
  - salsa trio
  - handmade tortillas
  - tacos
  - mole
  - fajitas
  - margaritas
  - bar
  - exterior
  - dining room
  - owner/team portrait
- Approve AI concierge boundaries.
