# El Molino Demo Script

Use this for a 6-8 minute pitch.

## 0:00 - Set The Frame

"I treated this as a new-chapter site, not just a redesign. The goal is to make the website match what guests are already noticing: new management, a warmer room, house salsas, fresh tortillas, margaritas, and a more confident guest path."

## 0:45 - Current Gap

"The current site makes the ownership change clear, which is important. But it mostly stops there. The menu is still a PDF, hours are not doing enough work on the page, and the new room and guest proof are not carrying the first impression."

## 1:30 - Hero

Show top of prototype.

"Here the guest gets the whole story quickly: El Molino, new Zepeda-family ownership and management, Carpentersville since 1984, renovated room, menu, call/directions, and live status."

## 2:15 - New Management

Show banner and story section.

"We do not hide the new management message. We make it clearer and warmer. Instead of only saying what it is not connected to, we say what it is now: a new Zepeda-family chapter of the same landmark."

## 3:00 - Menu

Show menu highlights.

"This is the biggest practical fix. Guests should not have to pinch a PDF. The full PDF can stay, but the site needs readable sections and eventually clear pricing/add-on language."

## 4:00 - Reviews

Show carousel.

"The review section is not a loud widget. It is a quiet proof layer: room, food, service, tortillas, bar. It reinforces the actual reasons people choose the restaurant."

## 5:00 - AI Concierge

Open "Ask the host."

Ask:

- "Are you open right now?"
- "What should I order first?"

Say:

"This is scoped as a host, not an order system. It answers simple guest questions and points anything exact or time-sensitive back to the restaurant."

## 6:30 - Visit / Mobile

Show visit section and sticky mobile actions.

"The mobile guest always has a next step: menu, call, or directions."

## Close

"This is pitch-ready as a direction. To launch, I would want owner signoff on final wording, menu/prices, photos, domain, and reservation/order links. But the core is here: the site now tells the right story and gives guests a much clearer path."
