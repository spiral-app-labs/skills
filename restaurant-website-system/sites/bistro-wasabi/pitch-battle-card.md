# Bistro Wasabi Pitch Battle Cards

Use these with the live prototype and the pitch doc. The tone is respectful: Bistro Wasabi is not broken. The restaurant already has demand, systems, and loyal diners. The opportunity is making the website match that reality.

## Core Position

> "Keep the reservation, carry-out, gift card, delivery, phone, email, and map paths. Rebuild the guest experience around how people actually choose dinner: menu, location, reserve, order, directions, call."

## Strongest Purchase Reasons

1. **It lowers friction.** Guests can get to menu, locations, reservations, carry-out, directions, and phone faster.
2. **It sounds like the restaurant.** The copy now reads like dinner, not an agency presentation.
3. **It turns reviews into on-site trust.** Guest quotes now support the same things the page sells: fresh sushi, martinis, the room, service, and repeat visits.
4. **It protects working systems.** No risky migration is required for reservations, carry-out, gift cards, delivery, phone, email, or maps.
5. **It makes the food specific.** Sushi, maki, starters, steaks, seafood, martinis, sake, wine, and weekly specials are visible.
6. **It justifies replacing two sites/pages.** The system can represent both locations cleanly while keeping a shared brand experience.

## Objection Counters

| If they say... | You say... |
| --- | --- |
| "Our regulars already know us." | "They do. This is for new diners, mobile visitors, gift card buyers, carry-out guests, and people deciding between dinner options." |
| "We do not want to look too fancy." | "Agreed. The site stays honest: sushi, fusion plates, martinis, wine specials, steaks, seafood, and two locations. No false omakase claim." |
| "Will this break ordering or reservations?" | "No. The current systems stay in place. The site simply gives guests a cleaner path into them." |
| "What if hours or menus differ by location?" | "Before launch we confirm those details and can split location content wherever needed." |
| "Why switch if the current site technically works?" | "Because technically working is not the same as making dinner decisions easy on mobile." |

## Release Guardrails

- Verify all live links before presenting or publishing.
- Confirm hours, menu, prices, and location differences with the owner.
- Confirm selected third-party review excerpts with the owner, and verify rating/count before adding any live number.
- Confirm photography ownership or replace with approved first-party assets.
- Do not claim formal omakase, awards, review counts, or rankings unless owner-approved and verified.

## Close

> "This is a cleaner guest path, not a risky rebuild. It makes Bistro Wasabi easier to choose, easier to reserve, easier to order, and easier to visit."
