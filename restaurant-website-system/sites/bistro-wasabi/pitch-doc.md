# Bistro Wasabi Pitch Doc

## 60-Second Read

Bistro Wasabi already has the ingredients a strong restaurant website needs: fresh sushi, martinis, wine specials, steaks and seafood for mixed tables, two Algonquin Road locations, reservations, carry-out, gift cards, delivery, phone, email, and directions.

The current site has those pieces, but guests have to work too hard to use them. The first screen is generic, the menu is difficult to scan on mobile, and the revenue actions feel like utility links instead of a clear dinner path.

This version keeps the systems they already use and turns the site into a cleaner front door: sushi, martinis, steaks, menu, locations, reservations, carry-out, weekly specials, and real guest reviews are all visible in plain guest language.

## Call Opener

> "You already have the food, the reputation, and the reservation/order systems. I built the version where a guest can land on the site and immediately know what to do next: view the menu, pick a location, reserve, order carry-out, or get directions."

## Why This Is Worth Switching

| Opportunity | Why It Matters | What The New Site Does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First screen | The current first screen does not clearly say sushi, martinis, steaks, two locations, or what action to take. | Leads with "Sushi, Martinis, Steaks," premium sushi imagery, and direct Reserve / Order / Menu actions. |
| Menu discovery | A guest choosing dinner wants fast category scanning, especially on mobile. | Adds menu highlights on the homepage and a native menu page with sections for sushi, starters, entrees/steaks, and drinks. |
| Location clarity | Two locations can create friction if actions are not split clearly. | Puts menu and locations directly below the hero, then gives Lake in the Hills and Hoffman Estates clear action cards. |
| Guest trust | Public reviews already say the sushi is fresh, the room feels polished, and people come back for years. | Adds a mobile-first, auto-scrolling guest review carousel with short excerpts and a calm, premium presentation. |
| Mobile conversion | Most guests arrive on a phone and need the next tap quickly. | Keeps Reserve, Carry Out, Directions, and Call fixed in the mobile thumb zone. |
| Restaurant voice | Copy should sound like a restaurant talking to diners, not a website audit. | Uses diner-facing language throughout: menu, locations, reviews, weekly specials, gift cards, and carry-out. |

## Final Homepage Flow

1. **Hero:** Bistro Wasabi, "Sushi, Martinis, Steaks," fresh sushi imagery, Reserve / Order Carry-out / View Menu.
2. **Plan your visit:** Menu and Locations cards immediately below the hero.
3. **Menu highlights:** Sushi, starters, steaks, and drinks with image-led category tiles.
4. **Visit:** Lake in the Hills and Hoffman Estates action cards.
5. **Guest reviews:** Short excerpts about fresh sushi, martinis, the room, service, and long-running consistency.
6. **House style:** Sushi-bar favorites, warm starters, martinis, wine specials, steaks, and seafood.
7. **Signature favorites:** Named menu items to make the food feel specific.
8. **Weekly specials:** Tuesday wine bottles, Wednesday martinis, Sunday Mai Tais.
9. **Dinner here or at home:** Gift card, delivery, and email actions.

## What To Show Live

1. Start on desktop and show the first viewport: premium sushi image, direct headline, and primary actions.
2. Scroll once to show that Menu and Locations come first, not buried halfway down the page.
3. Continue to the guest review carousel and show the guest language: fresh sushi, polished room, drinks, and repeat visits.
4. Open `/menu` and show the native menu sections and sticky category rail.
5. Open `/locations` and show the two-location action split.
6. Switch to mobile and show the sticky action bar: Reserve, Carry Out, Directions, Call.

## Positioning Line

> "This does not replace the systems you already trust. It keeps them, then makes the website feel like the same quality of night out as the restaurant."

## Guardrails Before Production

- Confirm hours before final schema and live open-status launch.
- Confirm whether Hoffman Estates shares the same hours, phone, menu, and online ordering setup.
- Re-verify reservation, carry-out, gift card, delivery, phone, email, and map links before launch.
- Owner-approve menu items, prices, and photography before publishing.
- Confirm the selected third-party review excerpts with the owner, and only add a live rating/count after day-of verification.

## Close

> "The restaurant already has the hard parts working. This gives guests a faster, more polished path to the table, the menu, carry-out, and both locations."
