# Battle Cards — MAAX Asian BBQ & Hot Pots

Owner-objection responses for the cold pitch. One objection per card, pull cold during the call.

---

## Card 1 — "Our website is fine"

**The truth they hear:** *"You're going to charge me to redo something that works."*

**Counter:**
> *"Open your phone right now and tap the 'Prices' link in your nav. It's a 404. For an AYCE concept, pricing is the conversion question — and your site doesn't answer it. The rest of the rebuild is bonus; the broken /prices page alone is worth fixing."*

**Backup:** `screenshots/prices-mobile.png` — the literal 404 screen.

---

## Card 2 — "We use OpenTable / we're locked in"

**The truth they hear:** *"You want me to switch reservation systems."*

**Counter:**
> *"OpenTable stays. The new homepage uses the same Reserve link you already have — guests still book through OpenTable, you still see them in your dashboard, your 4.8★/41-diner OpenTable history stays intact. We're refreshing the wrapper, not the engine."*

---

## Card 3 — "We don't have time for a project"

**The truth they hear:** *"This is going to drag."*

**Counter:**
> *"The site is already built — you can open the preview URL right now. If you approve, we point your domain at it and you're live in under a week. The only meaningful time on your end is confirming AYCE pricing numbers, which is a 5-minute call."*

---

## Card 4 — "How is this different from a Wix template?"

**Counter:**
> *"Three things a template can't do: (1) we read 600+ of your public Google reviews and built the homepage around the dishes guests actually mention by name — Szechuan spicy broth, malatang, beef short ribs. (2) We built an AI concierge grounded in your specific AYCE tiers, not a generic chatbot. (3) We added structured data so Google can render your hours and rating in search results. A template gives you a layout. We give you the format-explainer for first-timers."*

---

## Card 5 — "Who else have you done this for?"

**Counter:**
> *"Same template family for restaurants in the Schaumburg / Hoffman Estates / Arlington Heights corridor — heritage Italian, fine-dining sushi, family-trattoria, AYCE Asian. Happy to share a live-running comparable in your category. Your version uses the same proven base, with the AYCE pricing card and the four-step format explainer tuned to MAAX specifically."*

---

## Card 6 — "We're independent — we don't have a marketing budget like Skylark"

**The truth they hear:** *"I can't compete with Shabu-Yo's Schaumburg location two miles away."*

**Counter:**
> *"Exactly the point. Skylark's site is corporate-templated — same look on all 3,000 of their locations. Your site can be specifically about your room, your AYCE tiers, your 4.7★ neighborhood reputation. The independent angle IS the pitch — we're going to surface the things only your restaurant has, not the things every Asian restaurant has."*

---

## Card 7 — "What about chef-as-brand? We'd rather stay format-focused."

**Counter:**
> *"Totally fine. The current build leaves the chef out of the homepage and centers the format itself — 'Two interactive traditions, one table.' The AskConcierge speaks as 'the kitchen,' not as a person. If you change your mind later, we add a chef section in 30 minutes. The choice stays yours."*

---

## Card 8 — "AI concierge — what's it going to say?"

**Counter:**
> *"It's grounded in your AYCE tiers, your menu rotation, your hours, your phone. It will not invent menu items, prices, or hours. For allergies it tells the guest to call. For booking it sends them to OpenTable. We can show you the system prompt — every word it knows about MAAX is in one file you can read."*

**Backup:** `app/api/chat/route.ts` — show the SYSTEM_PROMPT.

---

## Card 9 — "We're not in Schaumburg, we're in Arlington Heights"

**Counter:**
> *"You're 4 miles from Woodfield Mall. The audience drawing to Woodfield is the same audience driving to your block. The new homepage explicitly says 'Arlington Heights — Woodfield Corridor' so SEO and ad targeting both pull from the right zip codes. Geography is a feature, not a problem."*

---

**Counter:**
