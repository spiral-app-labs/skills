# Galati's Hideaway — Pitch Battle Card

> Owner-facing pitch tool for Vince and Katie Galati. Use alongside the
> deploy-preview URL and the `audit.md` proof block. **Goal:** book the
> redesign without triggering "you don't understand my restaurant" defense.
> Tone is owner-pride first — Vince has been doing this 39 years; the
> website is the only thing underselling the work.

---

## The position (one line)

> *"Your kitchen has 1,107 reviews at 4.3 stars and you're #1 Italian in Cary on Restaurant Guru. Your homepage doesn't show a star, a number, or the year you opened. The site is the only thing underselling Galati's."*

This is **not** "your site is broken." It is "your site is hiding 34 years of work."

## Why these matter (the pre-emptive walkthrough)

1. **Open with the proof.** "If a diner Googles 'best Italian Cary' tonight, you're already #1 on Restaurant Guru and #5 of 38 on TripAdvisor. Your homepage doesn't tell them. The redesign does."
2. **Then the hideaway story.** "The line everyone leaves in their review — 'three atmospheres in one building' — is the most specific thing about Galati's. We made it a section."
3. **Then the paths.** "You've got five real revenue streams. The current site treats them all the same. The redesign picks Order, Plan a Party, and View Menu as the three things 90% of guests came for. Catering and gift cards live in the footer where buyers already look."
4. **Then the specials.** "Half-price pizza Monday, $3 tacos Tuesday, half-price wines Wednesday — this is the content that turns a first visit into a regular. We put it in a marquee ribbon and a 2x2 deal grid right after the trust strip."
5. **Then the family.** "Vince at 18 in Oshkosh. Varro's in Schaumburg in '87. Cary in '92. Spring Street '98–2014. Feinberg Court since. Same recipes, same family. We told that story on /about — verbatim from the audit."

## Strongest purchase reasons (use 2–3)

1. **Sells the order first.** Heartland link stays verbatim. Order Now is the primary CTA. The 6-tap pizza order becomes 1 tap.
2. **Surfaces 34 years of proof.** 1,107 reviews · #1 Italian · Since 1992 — every week these aren't on the homepage is a week of trust diners can't see.
3. **Specials drive repeats.** Monday/Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday/Friday/Saturday/Sunday all have a deal. The current site buries them on a sub-page; the redesign makes them a homepage feature.
4. **Three-atmosphere split lets guests self-select.** Quiet dining room, sports bar, patio — the existing noise complaint becomes a feature when you show guests they can pick their room.
5. **Preserves the entire stack.** Heartland POS, Flavorplate banquets, gift cards, social handles, Google reviews — nothing migrates.

## Proposed ask (the offer)

A focused redesign package:

- Strategy + register-honest redesign of the Cary site
- Homepage, menu, about, contact
- Mobile order path + Heartland handoff + click-to-call
- Live open-now indicator
- Restaurant JSON-LD (address, geo, hours, `acceptsReservations: false`, `priceRange: "$$"`, `aggregateRating`, social `sameAs`)
- Banquet inquiry section in front of existing Flavorplate form (no migration)
- Photography cleanup using existing first-party shots (Yelp + Google + reviewer-uploaded)
- One owner-voice revision round (specials prices, hours, staff names, founding details)

**What we explicitly do not do in v1:** migrate Heartland, migrate Flavorplate, build a new POS, manage social channels, run paid ads, re-shoot photography, redesign the catering PDF.
