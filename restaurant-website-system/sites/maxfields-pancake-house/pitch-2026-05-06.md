## Executive summary

Maxfield's already has strong local proof, but on May 6, 2026 the official domain was returning a Wix `ConnectYourDomain` 404 instead of a usable restaurant website. This preview fixes that basic credibility gap first. It gives Schaumburg diners a warmer, owner-controlled path to call, get directions, scan menu highlights, see public review proof, and handle uncertain details like hours truthfully instead of forcing them to piece everything together across Google, Yelp, Restaurantji, and scattered search results.

## Before / after in owner language

### Before

- The official Maxfield domain was broken, so people searching for the restaurant could hit a dead-end 404.
- Hours, ordering, and reservation expectations were unclear because public sources did not fully agree.
- Strong public proof existed, but it was scattered across Google Maps, Yelp, Restaurantji, and tourism/directory pages instead of living on one owner-controlled page.
- Menu confidence was split across third-party listings, snippets, and review mentions rather than presented in one clear breakfast-house story.

### After

- One warm Schaumburg breakfast-house preview now replaces the broken-domain story with a usable first impression.
- The preview is phone-first and directions-first, which fits a neighborhood breakfast restaurant better than pretending unsupported online flows exist.
- Public-source menu highlights now give diners a clearer sense of what Maxfield's is known for: pancakes, waffles, French toast, skillets, eggs, benedicts, omelettes, coffee, soup, burgers, sandwiches, and wraps.
- Review themes are summarized into trust proof without inventing quotes, and hours uncertainty is handled openly with a call-to-confirm handoff.
- A safe concierge layer answers common questions from a fixed Maxfield-specific knowledge base instead of making things up.

## Owner objections / truth-safe answer preview

### Why do I need this if Google already has our info?

Google helps discovery, but it does not replace an owner-controlled destination. Right now the official domain can send that demand into a Wix 404. This preview gives Maxfield's one place to catch that traffic and turn it into calls, directions, and visit confidence.

### What about hours?

This preview does not pretend the hours are settled when public sources conflict. It keeps the uncertainty visible and routes diners to the verified phone number instead of risking a wrong-hours promise.

### Can this handle ordering?

Not safely yet. Public sources suggest takeout/order activity, but a direct owner-controlled ordering provider was not verified. The preview stays truthful and pushes takeout questions to the phone until a real ordering path is confirmed.

### Is this AI going to make stuff up?

No. The concierge in this preview is restricted to a fixed Maxfield-specific knowledge base built from captured public facts and explicit fallback rules. When something is not confirmed, it says so and hands off to the phone number.

### Do we have to replace everything?

No. The sales story here is not "rip everything out." It is "stop losing trust on a broken domain and give diners a clean, accurate first stop." This can be a phased replacement or a first recovery layer.

## What is intentionally not claimed

- No fake exact hours
- No fake direct order provider
- No fake reservation flow
- No invented customer quotes
- No invented prices
- No invented awards
