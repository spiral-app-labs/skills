# Ciao Baby! pitch brief

- Pitch posture: clarify-and-convert
- Archetype: `gusto-01`
- Demo stance: warm heritage-Italian upgrade for a loved Barrington neighborhood restaurant with fragmented public-source identity

## Before: where the current web presence leaks trust

Ciao Baby already has real local love and recognizable menu proof, but the public web footprint makes a first-time guest work too hard:

- search/public sources surface both `ciaobabyonline.com` and `ciaobaby.net`, creating a two-domain trust problem before a guest even reads the menu
- the current/legacy source has useful facts but does not package the family-run warmth, private-party value, or review-backed favorites into a clear mobile sales path
- hours and domain ownership need verification, so guests should be handed to phone/directions instead of a risky fake booking or form flow
- off-site reviews are doing the emotional selling: warm bread, generous plates, lamb chops Vesuvio, burnt pasta, carrot cake, birthdays, and familiar service

## What guests already love

The review packet and audit support a very specific owner-safe story:

- old-school Italian hospitality that makes guests feel at home
- generous portions, warm bread, and family-meal comfort
- named menu hooks: lamb chops Vesuvio, CiaoBaby! Burnt Pasta, stuffed artichoke, Aunt Dodie’s eggplant, homemade gnocchi, chicken parmesan, salted caramel gelato, and carrot cake
- birthday/private-room occasions and staff who are remembered by name
- a downtown Barrington location that should be easier to call, visit, and trust from a phone

## After: what the redesign fixes

The preview turns those scattered facts into a coherent pre-visit sales path:

- the hero immediately says family-run Italian in downtown Barrington and pushes the safest conversion: call Ciao Baby
- the `gusto-01` layout preserves warm heritage Italian pacing instead of making the restaurant feel like generic luxury dining
- menu highlights distinguish source-backed priced rows from customer-mentioned favorites that require phone confirmation
- the contact page centers phone, directions, publicly listed hours, private-party proof, and source caveats without fake forms or reservations
- the truthful concierge answers from verified/local evidence only and refuses unsupported reservations, ordering, prices, allergy guidance, and private-event promises
- mobile polish adds quick access to Menu, Directions, and Parties while keeping the phone CTA prominent

## Strongest caveat

Do not pitch this as “Ciao Baby has no website.” The better owner-safe hook is that Ciao Baby has real local love and useful public facts, but the two-domain footprint and legacy presentation make trust and planning harder than they need to be. Also, final delivery still requires owner verification for preferred domain, exact current hours, and any changed menu pricing.

## 60-second demo path for Ethan

1. Open the home page and stop at the hero: “Family-run Italian in downtown Barrington,” call-first CTA, public hours caveat, and review-theme proof all land immediately.
2. Tap the mobile quick nav to show that Menu, Directions, and Parties are one tap away.
3. Open Menu and point to the owner-safe split between source-backed menu highlights and customer-mentioned favorites marked “Ask.”
4. Open Contact and show phone/directions, publicly listed hours, map, private-party proof, and the domain/source caveat handled without a fake form.
5. Show the truthful concierge refusing reservations/prices/allergy questions while still answering safe menu, hours, location, and private-party prompts.
6. Close with the owner line: “This does not change how Ciao Baby operates — it makes the web presence finally feel as warm, specific, and trustworthy as the restaurant people already describe.”
