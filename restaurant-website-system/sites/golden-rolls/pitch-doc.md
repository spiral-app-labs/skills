- Restaurant: Golden Rolls

- Selected archetype: Bamzi, adapted for a casual hidden-gem sushi/Japanese bistro, not formal omakase

## One-line pitch

Golden Rolls already has the proof of a beloved Woodstock sushi spot, but the current web experience undersells the room, the menu depth, and the one-tap call/directions path that turns hungry mobile visitors into orders.

## Why this is worth showing the owner

Golden Rolls is not a cold lead with no proof. The public evidence is strong:

- Google proof at capture: 4.6 from 348 reviews.
- Restaurantji proof: 4.7 from 187 ratings.
- Review themes: hidden-gem interior, fresh/generous sushi, creative roll depth, attentive service, and reliable takeout/delivery.
- Menu depth: special rolls, sushi, sashimi, teriyaki, tempura, steak, seafood, noodles, fried rice, hot appetizers, and non-raw options.
- Real conversion paths: dine-in, takeout, delivery, phone, directions, official site, Facebook, and hours.

The owner-facing angle is simple: guests already like the restaurant. The website should make new guests believe the reviews before they arrive.

## Current-site pain points

1. **The first impression is weaker than the restaurant proof.**
   The current owned site has real menu content, but the presentation feels dated and dense compared with the strength of the reviews.

2. **The hidden-gem interior is not sold enough.**
   Reviews repeatedly say not to let the outside scare you away and describe the inside as clean, calm, lovely, and surprisingly nice. The new build turns that into the main story.

3. **Mobile conversion needs to be obvious.**
   A hungry guest should not have to hunt for phone, directions, hours, takeout, delivery, or menu categories. The preview keeps those actions visible.

4. **The dense menu needs a better scan path.**
   Golden Rolls has enough menu breadth to satisfy sushi lovers and mixed groups, but the old structure makes that depth harder to absorb quickly.

## What the preview fixes

### 1. Better first impression

The hero now leads with the real hook: “The hidden sushi room on Eastwood Drive.” It positions Golden Rolls as a calm local Japanese bistro with fresh sushi, generous rolls, and attentive service.

### 2. Review-backed trust

The preview uses safe, evidence-backed themes from the Google Highest review packet:

- hidden-gem interior
- fresh/generous sushi
- creative rolls
- friendly service
- takeout/delivery reliability

No fake chef credentials, no luxury omakase claims, no invented sourcing, and no fake reservation path.

### 3. One-tap conversion

Phone, directions, menu, hours, dine-in, takeout, and delivery context are kept close to the user on mobile. The contact page avoids a fragile blank map embed and uses a stable directions/details handoff.

### 4. Menu clarity

The menu page adds category shortcuts and guest-facing recommendation blocks for:

- Godzilla / Mini Godzilla
- hot starters and non-raw options
- nigiri and sashimi cuts

This makes Golden Rolls feel easier to order from without flattening the full Japanese menu.

### 5. Safer AI concierge

The AI concierge is now limited to verified Golden Rolls facts and safe handoffs. It can help with menu ideas, hours, directions, and takeout/delivery basics, but it asks guests to call for allergies, reservations, large parties, specials, delivery availability, and time-sensitive questions.
