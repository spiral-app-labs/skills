# Golden Rolls — Battle Cards

- Restaurant: Golden Rolls, Woodstock, IL
- Current site: https://goldenrollssushi.com/
- Preview stage: local build/evidence complete; deployed preview URL still pending packaging
- Archetype: Bamzi, adapted for a casual hidden-gem sushi/Japanese bistro

## Core sales frame

Golden Rolls already has strong customer proof. The job is not to invent a new brand. The job is to make the website match what guests already say: fresh generous sushi, creative rolls, a surprisingly nice dining room, attentive service, and easy takeout/delivery decisions.

Use this line when the conversation gets scattered:

> “Your best reviews are already selling the restaurant. This preview turns those reviews into the first impression new guests see on their phones.”

## Demo path

1. **Homepage**
   - Show the hidden-gem hook: “The hidden sushi room on Eastwood Drive.”
   - Point to one-tap Call and View menu CTAs.
   - Explain that the homepage sells the interior and review proof before guests judge the storefront.

2. **Proof / service strip**
   - Show the 4.6 / 348 Google review proof and service cards.
   - Explain that hours, phone-first ordering, and directions are visible without hunting.

3. **Menu page**
   - Show category shortcuts and review-backed picks.
   - Explain that the dense menu becomes easier for new guests, families, and mixed groups.

4. **Contact page**
   - Show phone, address, official site, and directions card.
   - Explain that the map handoff is reliable and avoids blank embed risk.

5. **Concierge**
   - Ask “What should I order tonight?” and then “Are you gluten free?”
   - Explain that it helps with safe basics and hands off for allergies/current availability.

## Objection cards

### “We already have a website.”

**Response:**
“Yes, and the existing site has real menu content. The gap is first impression and mobile clarity. This preview does not replace what makes Golden Rolls work. It makes the phone, menu, directions, hours, takeout, and review proof easier for a new guest to trust quickly.”

**Proof to cite:**
- Current-site audit notes dated/dense visual presentation and dense mobile navigation.
- Browser audit shows the real full menu exists, but the sellable issue is presentation and conversion.

**Show:**
Homepage hero, menu category shortcuts, contact card.

### “Our reviews already bring people in.”

**Response:**
“That is exactly why this is a good fit. The reviews are strong, but many new guests see the website before they commit. The preview makes those review themes visible immediately: hidden-gem interior, fresh generous sushi, creative rolls, and attentive service.”

**Proof to cite:**
- Google: 4.6 from 348 reviews at capture.
- Highest-filter packet: 30 written reviews captured.
- Review themes doc.

**Show:**
Home proof cards and What guests say block.

### “We use delivery apps / people call us anyway.”

**Response:**
“Great. The preview supports phone-first behavior instead of fighting it. It makes calling, directions, takeout, delivery context, and menu scanning easier on mobile, then hands time-sensitive questions back to the restaurant.”

**Proof to cite:**
- Public evidence supports dine-in, takeout, and delivery, but current availability should be confirmed by phone.
- Concierge QA routes delivery/current timing questions to phone.

**Show:**
Sticky/mobile nav, contact page, concierge delivery prompt.

### “I do not want AI making promises to guests.”

**Response:**
“Agreed. The concierge is intentionally conservative. It answers only verified basics and sends guests to the phone for allergies, reservations, large parties, specials, delivery availability, current wait times, and anything time-sensitive.”

**Proof to cite:**
- Concierge QA transcript.
- Fallback answers when the API key is unavailable.
- Prompt rules prohibit fake reservations, allergy advice, provider assumptions, and invented details.

**Show:**
Ask “Are you gluten free?” and “Can I reserve a table for 8 tonight?”

### “We do not want to look too fancy.”

**Response:**
“That is why the route is Bamzi adapted for a casual local sushi bistro, not luxury omakase. It is polished and cinematic, but the copy stays grounded in real guest language: fresh rolls, generous portions, clean interior, friendly service, and easy ordering.”

**Proof to cite:**
- Website-agency archetype choice: Bamzi, not Qitchen/omakase.
- Hard bans observed: no fake chef, no fake omakase, no invented sourcing.

**Show:**
Homepage headline and About page.

### “How do we know this helps orders?”

**Response:**
“The preview focuses on the actions closest to revenue: call, menu, directions, takeout, delivery, and current availability. It does not ask guests to learn a complicated brand story before they act.”

**Proof to cite:**
- Phone CTA appears in header/footer and hero.
- Directions and address appear across contact/footer.
- Menu page has scan-friendly categories and recommendations.

**Show:**
Mobile screenshots and live header/footer CTAs.

### “The menu/prices change.”

**Response:**
“That is why the site should be structured for easy updates, and the concierge does not promise current specials. Pricing came from captured public menu evidence and should be checked before final delivery.”

**Proof to cite:**
- Pitch doc caveat says menu/pricing should be rechecked.
- Concierge routes specials/current availability to phone.

**Show:**
Menu page and concierge specials/delivery handoff.

## Founder cautions

- Do not say the preview is deployed until a preview URL exists.
- Do not imply Golden Rolls has online reservations.
- Do not promise allergy safety, current specials, delivery provider availability, or holiday/current hours.
- Do not call the restaurant omakase or luxury. Say polished local Japanese bistro / hidden-gem sushi room.
- If asked about price accuracy, say public menu evidence was used and final pricing should be checked before launch.

## Close

“If nothing else, this makes the first 30 seconds better. A new guest sees what existing fans already know: Golden Rolls is more inviting inside than the outside suggests, the sushi has real depth, and calling or finding the restaurant is easy.”
