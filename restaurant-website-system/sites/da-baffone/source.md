## Implementation Positioning

This fork keeps Gusto's warm dark canvas, italic display serif, testimonial overlay, hover image treatment, and editorial menu layout, but changes the conversion strategy to phone-first reservations. The scrollable homepage is the default pitch because it is more familiar to restaurant owners; the compact cinematic version tests appetite for the more aesthetic Gusto interpretation.

## Secret Sauce

- Owner/site claim: Da Baffone is a family-owned Southern Italian restaurant in downtown Crystal Lake, established in 2010, rooted in old-world recipes, fresh ingredients, wine, and a connection to Santo Stefano del Sole, Italy.
- Review/press-confirmed strengths: warm brick dining room, cozy special-dinner/date-night feel without stiffness, Southern Italian sauces, pasta, seafood, desserts, martinis, wine, and strong Best Italian / Best Wine Bar local proof.
- Named items or experiences: Chicken Piccata, pasta, seafood, wine and martinis, desserts, catering, phone-first reservations, and the "good food, good wine, good friends" table promise.
- Guest language worth echoing: "cozy," "intimate," "welcoming," "family-run," "good food and good wine," and "special dinner."
- Risks / do-not-overclaim: Do not say OpenTable is active. Do not quote exact review totals or awards without fresh verification. Owner/table-time review nuance should be understood before the sales call, but not used as the pitch.
- Website/pitch implications: Lead with earned pride: the restaurant already has the reputation, room, awards, and food. The redesign makes the site behave more like the host: warm, clear, proud, and ready to help someone call or visit.
