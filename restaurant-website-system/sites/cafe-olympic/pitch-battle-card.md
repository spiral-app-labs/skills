## The position (one line)

> *"Your cafe is a 4.6-star Crystal Lake breakfast ritual that goes back to 1984 — and your website doesn't show a star, the cinnamon roll, the Greek skillet, or the Angelos farewell letter that makes the brand the brand. The site is the only thing underselling you."*

This is **not** "your site is broken." It is "your site is hiding what you've already earned."

## Why these matter (the pre-emptive walkthrough)

1. **Open with the proof.** "If a Sunday-morning diner Googles 'breakfast Crystal Lake' tonight, you're already 4.6 stars across 1,169 reviews. Your homepage doesn't tell them. The redesign does."
2. **Then the dishes by name.** "Cinnamon roll, Greek skillet, Wednesday pasticio. They're what every regular orders. They're now the second thing every visitor reads."
3. **Then the Greek thread.** "The cafe was Greek-owned for 21 years. The skillet and the pasticio are still the menu spine. The redesign says so — quietly. No theme-park, no Greek flag, just typography and a small section divider."
4. **Then the handoff letter.** "Chris and Nancy's letter is already public. We made it the trust spine of the new site, paired with your welcome. Long-time regulars need to see this loop closed."
5. **Then the menu and the call path.** "Inline HTML menu replaces the print JPG. A phone can read it. Tap-to-call sticky button on mobile so a Sunday driver can decide in one tap."
6. **Close on the AI host.** "There's a small 'Ask the host' button on every page. It knows your menu, your hours, your story. Trained on your reviews and your Shaw Local coverage. Never invents a price."

## Strongest purchase reasons (use 2–3)

1. **Surfaces 1,900+ five-star reviews you've already earned.** The fastest credibility lift on the property — and one of the lowest-effort changes for the owner.
2. **Replaces the JPG menu with one a phone can read.** Same items, same prices, same Toast for ordering. Just visible to search, AI, and accessibility tools for the first time.
3. **Closes the Angelos→current-owner trust loop.** The "are these the people I remember" question every long-time regular asks gets answered in one page.
4. **Mobile call path that actually works.** A Sunday-morning driver gets one tap from "is it open" to dialing the cafe. The current site is two screens of scrolling and a copy-paste.
5. **AI host carries the cafe's voice.** Answers the most common questions in the team's tone. Saves the counter from picking up the phone for "are you open."
6. **Preserves what's working.** Toast Tab for online order stays. WordPress is replaced (faster, more secure, easier on the team), but no platform you actually use day-to-day is touched.

## Fact guardrails (DO NOT pitch unverified)

- **Do not claim a "Best of" award.** Search returned nothing. Ask the owner before quoting any award.
- **Do not name chef Jon Vasquez on the public site** until staff status is reconfirmed (last verified Oct 2019).
- **Do not name Caitlyn (or any server) on the public site** without written permission.
- **Do not pitch the prices as final.** The build's prices are reasonable defaults derived from the menu; hold for owner sign-off before launch.
- **Do not overclaim "third generation."** The audit found three ownership eras (1984-founders → Angelos 1998 → current 2019), not three family generations. Pre-flight clarify with owner.
- **Do not quote the Angelos farewell letter on the new site without owner sign-off.** It's already public on Shaw Local — but the new site reproducing it is a different consent question.
- **Do not promise the AI host will never hallucinate.** Position it as "answers from a verified fact sheet, defers to phone for anything time-sensitive." That's true and defensible.
