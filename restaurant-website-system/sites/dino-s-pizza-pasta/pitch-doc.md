# Dino's Pizza & Pasta — Owner Pitch

## One-line pitch

Dino’s already has the food reputation; this preview turns that reputation into a call-first, menu-first website that feels like the Lake in the Hills neighborhood pizzeria regulars describe in reviews.

## What the current site is costing them

- The owned homepage is thin and exposes template/development debris like `AVIATOR`, `LONDON`, and `/menu-aviator`.
- The real strengths — crispy made-from-scratch thin crust, stuffed deep dish, pasta dinners, garlic bread sandwiches, beer/bar seating, and family warmth — are buried instead of leading the first impression.
- Mobile visitors have to work too hard to answer the basic conversion questions: what to order, when they are open, where they are, and how to call/menu quickly.

## What the preview fixes

1. **Stronger first impression.** The hero immediately says what Dino’s is: thin crust first, deep dish when the table wants more, pasta and beer behind it.
2. **Real menu packaging.** The preview turns the official menu into digestible sections: pizza lanes, regular favorites, more-than-pizza, hours, and visit context.
3. **Google proof above generic claims.** Captured Google evidence now powers the proof band: visible 4.5 rating, 252 reviews, 30 Highest-filter written reviews, and a real quote about Dino/family warmth.
4. **Mobile conversion polish.** Sticky mobile nav now includes `Menu` and `Call`; decorative clutter was removed from the mobile hero.
5. **Safe concierge layer.** The preview includes a source-locked concierge that answers common questions and hands off to call/menu/directions without inventing orders, waits, reservations, or delivery promises.
