# Port Edward Prospect Pitch

## Position

Port Edward does not need a "your website is dead" pitch. The current site is active and has the important pieces: menu content, Toast ordering, events, brunch, Dockside, loyalty, gift cards, reservations, private rooms, and a real story.

The problem is sorting. Guests have to assemble dinner, brunch, Dockside, private rooms, events, and ordering from a busy WordPress surface before they understand why Port Edward is special.

## Pitch Sentence

Port Edward already has what most restaurants wish they had: the Fox River, Mr. Ed's 1964 story, Z carrying the legacy forward, Sunday champagne brunch, Dockside, and rooms people remember. I rebuilt the site so guests can choose dinner, brunch, Dockside, private events, Toast ordering, or a phone-first reservation path without sorting through a busy utility stack.

## What The Rebuild Shows

- `PORT EDWARD` as the first brand signal.
- `Fox River seafood since 1964` as the position.
- Phone-first reservations preserved: `(847) 658-5441`.
- Toast ordering preserved as the secondary CTA.
- A segmented chooser for seafood dinner, champagne brunch, holiday brunch, Porpoise/Windmill seating, Dockside, and private events.
- Real page-native menu content, with seafood signatures visible quickly.
- Google proof without reviewer names: aggregate rating, topic chips, and short review excerpts only.
- Current event and seasonal paths for Spring Wine Tasting, Surf & Turf Buffet, Mother's Day Brunch, recurring brunch, live entertainment, and Dockside season state.
- A floating AI concierge that answers guest sorting questions while routing reservations, availability, allergies, and private-event details back to the phone.
- Restaurant JSON-LD with address, phone, hours, menu URL, Toast order URL, social links, and reservations.

## Why This Fits Port Edward

The rebuild keeps the texture that guests actually talk about: Fox River views, Dockside, the nautical room, fish/koi memories, Porpoise boat, windmill, brunch, crab legs, scallops, walleye, lobster, Port Platter, and named-staff warmth.

It avoids turning Port Edward into a pristine coastal resort. The visual tone is warm, riverfront, slightly eccentric, and heritage-minded.

## Concierge Angle

The AI concierge is not a reservation replacement. It is a sorting layer for mobile guests:

- "Do I need to call for brunch?"
- "Is Dockside open?"
- "What should I order for seafood dinner?"
- "Can I host a private event?"

Every high-intent path still routes to the real revenue action: call `(847) 658-5441`, order via Toast, open `/private-events`, or check `/events`.

## Owner-Safe Framing

"You have the content. You have the story. You have the revenue paths. This version just makes the first 10 seconds clearer for a phone guest."
