# Port Edward Pitch Battle Card

## Core Angle

The current site is alive but crowded. The rebuild makes the same active pieces easier to choose: dinner, brunch, Dockside, private events, Toast ordering, events, loyalty, gift cards, Google proof, and AI-guided guest sorting.

## Lead With

- Since 1964 on the Fox River.
- Phone-first reservations, not a forced booking engine.
- Sunday champagne brunch and holiday brunch as first-class products.
- Dockside season state surfaced early.
- Real menu proof: Port Platter, scallops, walleye, king crab, lobster, crab cakes.
- Anonymous Google proof: 4.2 rating, 2,278 reviews, review-topic chips, no reviewer names.
- Private rooms with clear capacities.
- Z and named staff warmth as trust texture.
- AI concierge as a helper, not a closer: it answers sorting questions and sends high-intent guests to the phone, Toast, events, or private-event page.

## Avoid

- "Your site is broken."
- "You need OpenTable/Resy."
- Overstating reviews or inventing testimonials.
- Using reviewer names in the prospect proof section.
- Luxury coastal language that makes the restaurant feel less like Port Edward.
- Hiding Toast, loyalty, gift cards, events, or Dockside because they are not "fine dining."
- Letting the AI concierge confirm reservations, allergy safety, private-room availability, or current pricing.

## Likely Objections

### "Our current site already works."

Yes. This pitch is not that the site is inactive. It is that the active pieces are spread across sliders, cards, menus, events, and footer links. The rebuild keeps those pieces and gives mobile guests a clearer first decision.

### "We take reservations by phone."

Good. The new version preserves the phone path and makes the call smarter by separating dinner, brunch, holidays, Porpoise/Windmill seating, Dockside, and private rooms.

### "Dockside is seasonal."

Exactly. The site should say that upfront instead of making guests hunt. The prospect shows Dockside as a seasonal service with a clear reopening state.

### "We already have Toast."

The rebuild keeps Toast as a strong secondary path and does not replace the POS-owned ordering flow.

### "We are not a fancy resort."

Correct. The fork was deliberately downshifted from the source template into Fox River heritage seafood: cream paper, river ink, red Dockside energy, old-room story, and real Port Edward service paths.

### "Will AI make stuff up?"

The concierge prompt is constrained to Port Edward facts, menu highlights, service rules, review themes, and phone-first guardrails. It cannot confirm availability or reservations. For serious allergies, deposits, current pricing, room terms, or urgent timing, it sends the guest to `(847) 658-5441`.

## Demo Walkthrough

1. Open the homepage and point to the first-screen choices.
2. Click `Plan Your Visit`.
3. Switch the chooser between dinner, brunch, Dockside, Porpoise/Windmill, and private events.
4. Open the Google review proof section and note that it uses aggregate/topic proof without names.
5. Open the AI concierge and ask: `Is Dockside open?`
6. Open `/menu` and show page-native seafood and brunch content.
7. Open `/private-events` and show room capacities.
8. Open `/events` and show current/seasonal aliveness.

## Close

"You do not need fewer pieces. You need the pieces you already have arranged around how guests decide on a phone."
