## The three outcomes we're selling

1. **Turn the under-construction page into a sales asset.** The current public site confirms the address, wine/spirit market, and small plates — but it does not show the atmosphere, reviews, visit paths, or concierge help that make Winestock worth choosing.
2. **Make the Winestock identity obvious fast.** The new Bramble preview leads with the real concept: Woodstock Square wine market + lounge, hand-selected bottles, boards/bites, live-music energy, and easy visit actions.
3. **Give guests safe, useful next steps.** No fake reservations or ordering. The site routes people to email, phone, Facebook updates, directions, bottle help, boards, and a truthful concierge that hands off anything uncertain.

## What we changed for Winestock

### 1. Replaced the "new online experience coming" gap with a full first impression

The current scraped site says: *"We're building our new online experience."* That is honest, but it leaves a lot of value hidden. The preview turns the same public facts into a complete landing page: address, wine market, small plates, flatbreads, sandwiches, charcuterie/cheese boards, Facebook updates, directions, and clear ways to contact the team.

### 2. Used the right template archetype: Bramble

Winestock is not a fine-dining steakhouse or a generic neighborhood restaurant. It is a bar/lounge/market concept with personality, music, regulars, wine, and boards. The selected `bramble-01` structure fits that: mood-forward, social, drink-led, and built around atmosphere instead of generic menu blocks.

### 3. Surfaced real review proof

The Google Reviews Highest packet captured a 4.9-star snapshot across 52 reviews, including 30 written Highest-sort reviews. The preview uses that proof carefully: review stats, exact excerpts from the attached packet, and repeated themes — friendly owner/staff, helpful wine guidance, live music, craft beer, charcuterie/cheese plates, and a relaxed local-room feeling.

### 4. Made the homepage answer the guest's actual question: "Why would I go?"

The top sections now frame three concrete visit reasons:

- **Market:** hand-selected bottles and pairing help.
- **Lounge:** wine, craft beer, conversation, and music.
- **Boards + bites:** small plates, flatbreads, sandwiches, charcuterie, and cheese boards for the lounge or home.

That is stronger than a generic "welcome to our restaurant" message because it matches the public evidence.

### 5. Added practical visit handoffs

The `VisitPlanner` section gives visitors three real paths:

- Ask for bottle picks by email.
- Check Facebook for live-music/lounge updates.
- Ask about boards.

These are intentionally grounded in public handoffs. There is no invented ordering provider, no fake reservation path, and no table-hold promise.

### 6. Added a truthful Winestock concierge

The concierge is deterministic and safe for a preview. Guests can ask about:

- hours snapshot,
- location and directions,
- bottle/pairing help,
- small plates and boards,
- live music and vibe,
- best contact path.

It refuses or safely hands off unsupported requests: reservations, table holds, ordering, allergies, private events, current prices/specials, and live availability. For those, it routes to email, phone, Facebook, or directions.

### 7. Mobile and 320px checks are already part of the work

The preview has browser evidence for desktop, 390px mobile, and 320px mobile. The latest checks found no fatal horizontal overflow, broken layout, fake form, or broken concierge blockers. Some secondary copy remains dense on small screens, which is a normal QA note for the next rounds.

## What stays the same

- **Business identity:** Winestock Market & Lounge stays a wine market + lounge, not a fake fine-dining rebrand.
- **Public contact paths:** email, phone, Facebook, and directions stay the conversion paths until ownership confirms anything else.
- **Menu truth:** the site only uses public categories already confirmed: hand-selected wines/spirits, small plates, flatbreads, sandwiches, charcuterie, and cheese boards.
- **No fake providers:** no invented reservations, online ordering, delivery, ticketing, or event booking.
- **No invented claims:** review quotes and stats come from the attached Google Reviews Highest packet.

## What we'd ask Winestock for before v2 / final launch

1. **Confirm current hours** before final delivery; the preview uses the Google snapshot captured during evidence collection.

3. **Share real interior / bottle / boards / music photos** if they want to replace the temporary mood imagery.
4. **Confirm whether any reservation, event, or private-party process exists** before adding those flows.
5. **Confirm menu details and any current prices** before publishing specific items or pricing.
