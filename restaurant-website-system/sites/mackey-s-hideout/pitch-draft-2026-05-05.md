## One-line pitch

Mackey's already has the hard part — a real live-music roadhouse following — but the current website presents it like a long event archive instead of a place people can instantly understand, trust, and choose for tonight.

## Founder-facing summary

Mackey's Hideout is not a generic restaurant lead. It is a McHenry live-music bar with open mic, karaoke, craft beer, whiskey, friendly regulars, and outdoor-show energy. Public proof is strong: Google shows a 4.6 rating from 408 reviews, Restaurantji shows 4.7 from 155 ratings, Restaurant Guru ranks it highly as a local pub/bar, and the highest-rating Google reviews repeatedly call out live music, hidden-gem dive/roadhouse atmosphere, friendly bartenders, craft beer, and the kind of crowd people come back for.

The current owned site has real information, especially the schedule, but it undersells the venue. It buries the reason to go behind a raw list of shows and does not package the bar story, drink credibility, directions, tickets, phone, hours, or proof into a strong first impression.

The new Bramble route turns Mackey's into a mood-forward venue page: big identity, real show art, schedule/ticket/beer CTAs, conservative food wording, proof from reviews, and a clear plan-your-visit path.

## What Ethan can say live

> "You already have a real scene here. The issue isn't that Mackey's lacks personality — it's that the site doesn't show it fast enough. We rebuilt the page around what people already love: live music, open mic, karaoke, craft beer, friendly regulars, and the hidden-roadhouse feel. The goal is simple: help someone land on the site and immediately know what's happening, how to get there, what to click next, and why this place is worth the drive."

## The before/after story

### Before

- Official site is reachable and information-rich, but reads like a schedule archive.
- Live music is present, but the bar/roadhouse identity is not shaped into a compelling first impression.
- Important conversion paths are scattered: schedule, beer menu, TicketWeb, address, phone, hours, and directions.
- Public review proof is strong but not surfaced on the owned site.
- Food evidence is inconsistent, so the site must avoid sounding like a full kitchen or restaurant-menu build.

### After

- Bramble-style hero creates instant venue mood using real Mackey show art.
- CTAs match actual Mackey behavior: `Shows`, `Beer`, tickets, directions, and phone.
- Review themes become proof: live music, craft beer, friendly staff, regular crowd, open mic/karaoke, dive/roadhouse appeal.
- Food stays truthful and cautious: frozen pizza, pizza/bar bites, or outside-food support only where evidence allows.
- Mobile experience is cleaner, with the hero wordmark fixed and no page-level horizontal overflow in the smoke check.

## Primary conversion paths

1. **See live music schedule** — official site schedule remains the source for shows.
2. **Open beer menu** — official beer-menu page supports drink credibility.
3. **Get outdoor-show tickets** — TicketWeb CTA only for the verified Concert by the Creek/outdoor-show link.
4. **Call Mackey's** — direct phone for owner-confirmation gaps, food/hours questions, and event details.
5. **Get directions** — McHenry location needs a fast path because reviews mention hidden/roadhouse/out-of-the-way character.
