## Positioning card

**Core claim:** Mackey's does not need a generic restaurant website. It needs a music-first bar/venue page that turns its real local proof into fast visitor decisions: shows, beer, tickets, phone, and directions.

**Best words:** hideout, roadhouse, live music, open mic, karaoke, craft beer, friendly regulars, worth the drive.

**Avoid:** upscale dining, reservations, full kitchen, polished cocktail lounge, generic nightlife, every-show tickets, owner-confirmed hours.

## Objection: "We already have a website."

**Response:** Yes — and that is exactly why this can be a low-friction improvement. The current site has valuable schedule information, but it behaves like a raw archive. The redesign keeps the useful links while making the first impression match what people already say in reviews: live music, drinks, friendly staff, and a hidden-gem roadhouse feel.

**Proof to cite:** Official site has extensive 2026 schedule; Google/Restaurantji/Restaurant Guru proof shows the stronger bar/music identity that the owned site underplays.

## Objection: "We're more of a bar than a restaurant."

**Response:** Correct, and the build respects that. It does not pretend Mackey's is a full-menu restaurant. It routes to Bramble because Mackey's is a bar/live-music venue. Food copy stays conservative and only references verified light support like frozen pizza, pizza/bar bites, or outside-food friendliness.

**Proof to cite:** Reviews conflict on food: some say no food/outside food, some mention frozen pizza or pizza/appetizers. That is why the site should not overclaim food.

## Objection: "People come for the bands, not the site."

**Response:** The site is not replacing the scene — it helps new people join it faster. A better homepage can answer the first-visit questions immediately: what's happening, where is it, how do I get tickets if needed, what kind of place is this, and should I bring/call about food?

**Proof to cite:** Highest-rating reviews repeatedly mention live music, open mic, karaoke, friendly crowd, and worth-the-drive energy.

## Objection: "We don't want something too fancy."

**Response:** This is not fancy for fancy's sake. The Bramble route keeps the rough-edged music-bar personality: show art, big identity, dark sections, and direct CTAs. It should feel like Mackey's online, not like a hotel bar or corporate venue.

**Proof to cite:** Review language: "ultimate dive bar," "quintessential road house," "diamond in the rough," "hidden" — the design should elevate that without sanding it down.

## Objection: "Our schedule changes a lot."

**Response:** The redesign can keep the official schedule as the source of truth instead of duplicating every event manually. The homepage can route visitors to the schedule, highlight the weekly rhythm, and provide ticket links only where verified.

**Proof to cite:** Existing official site already maintains the live music schedule and TicketWeb links; the new page improves routing to those assets.

## Objection: "Hours/food details are tricky."

**Response:** Agreed. The current scaffold treats hours as reported/provisional and avoids a live open/closed claim. Before delivery, owner-confirmed hours and food language should be locked. Until then, the safest CTA is call before you roll in.

**Proof to cite:** Restaurantji and Restaurant Guru corroborate hours, but owner confirmation is still pending. Reviews have mixed food descriptions.

## Objection: "Why mention reviews?"

**Response:** Because the reviews provide the clearest version of what makes Mackey's sellable. The site should not invent personality; it should surface the personality customers already describe.

**Proof to cite:** 30 written Highest-filter Google reviews captured; visible Google rating 4.6 from 408 reviews.

## Risk card

**Risk:** Over-selling food or hours.

**Mitigation:** Keep food cautious; mark hours as reported until owner-confirmed; use call CTA.

**Risk:** Bramble looks too cocktail-lounge if not personalized.

**Mitigation:** Use real Mackey show art, roadhouse language, outdoor-show/ticket context, beer/open-mic/karaoke proof.

**Risk:** Current stage mismatch with MC.
