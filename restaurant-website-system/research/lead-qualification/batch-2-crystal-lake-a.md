### #5 Da Baffone Cucina Italiana — Crystal Lake, IL (downtown, 111 N Main St)
- **Visual fit:** ✓ — rustic warm Italian trattoria: exposed Tuscan brick, small intimate bar-room up front, small main dining room behind, family-run vibe; reviews and photos consistently describe "cozy," "intimate," "warm" without tipping into white-tablecloth fine-dining. Matches gusto-01's heritage-Italian-trattoria register cleanly.
- **Menu register:** $$-$$$ (avg entrée $36.55 from 10 samples; cheapest pasta $24.95 Gnocchi/Ravioli/Spaghetti & Meatballs; priciest $52.95 Duo a la Gorgonzola and $50.95 Frutta di Mare; Chicken/Veal Marsala/Picatta $26.95-$32.95; Salmone $32.95; Braciola $32.95). Clear $$-$$$ — sits at the upper end of gusto-01's range and is a better register fit than Cucina Bella (batch 1) which was $$ low-end.
- **Review tone:** "cozy, upbeat, welcoming," "reliably outstanding family-run," "authentic Italian," "intimate without the volume of larger restaurants," "wonderful gem." 289 Yelp reviews, 922 Facebook reviews with 96% recommend rate. Best Italian + Best Wine Bar McHenry County. Heritage-authentic tone with an upscale-intimate lean — perfect gusto register.
- **Current site:** 4/10 — WordPress + WooCommerce (gift cards). Functional but dated execution: phone-first reservations on homepage copy, checked OpenTable listing is not bookable, menus live across separate pages, no strong reservation CTA above fold, WordPress-era photography carousel feels 2015. Site fails to convey the local-proof authority or the $36.55 avg entrée price tier — visually underrepresents what they actually are.
- **Switch reason:** CONCRETE — "homepage treats reservations like a phone note instead of a primary mobile action; hero photography/typography reads as $$ casual when actual menu and review-set are $$-$$$ upscale-trattoria — site is costing them the premium impression they've earned."
- **Switching cost:** medium — phone-first reservations, WooCommerce gift cards, email/map/social links can be preserved; WordPress swap is straightforward; 15+ yrs of operating content to port but not integration-heavy.
- **Pitch test:** ✓ — gusto-01's heritage Italian trattoria aesthetic with warm wood/brick and slideshow-capable hero would represent the room faithfully AND elevate the price-tier signal. Honest fit.
- **Decision:** BUILD (priority). Highest-conviction in batch. Prior memo's "high-conviction gusto fit" flag confirmed.
- **Notes:** Textbook register match + concrete site failures + phone-first reservation clarity can work without claiming an online booking provider. Biggest risk is owner-operator emotional attachment to current WordPress site (15 yrs of family-business content). Pitch must lead with "you've already earned trust — your site is the only thing underselling you." Polarized reviews include owner-conduct complaints about table-time limits; not a site issue but worth knowing before sales conversation.

Reviews themselves don't emphasize gaming (0 of 4 TripAdvisor reviews mention it), but the gaming-lounge exists and would show up in any photo-research the owner does of the proposed mockup.

Operates at the UPPER end of plate-01's $$ and arguably stretches into varro's $$-$$$. Needs actual menu PDF price confirmation before locking template.
- **Review tone:** "hidden gem," "phenomenal dining experience," "top notch," "upscale appearance," "incredible steaks and pastas," "huge portions." Upscale-casual bistro tone dominates; gaming is conspicuously absent from review discourse. Only 89 Yelp reviews + 4 Tripadvisor — newer establishment (est 2024), modest review volume.
- **Current site:** 5/10 — SpotHopper platform (target platform to displace!), OpenTable integrated, responsive carousel, professional food photography. Functional but SpotHopper-cookie-cutter execution; no above-the-fold hero narrative, menu prices hidden behind additional navigation, generic layout that doesn't differentiate from the other 100+ SpotHopper-built McHenry County bars/restaurants.
- **Switch reason:** CONCRETE — "SpotHopper template is visually indistinguishable from every other McHenry County bar/grill they compete with; menu prices are hidden behind two clicks which is costing them the 'upscale-small-plates' positioning their food/room actually delivers." SpotHopper-displacement is on-thesis for the agency.
- **Switching cost:** medium-high — SpotHopper is sticky (handles site + reservations + online ordering + sometimes POS), would need clean migration path; OpenTable already external so that survives; Instagram/FB presence active; 2024-founded means limited legacy content.
- **Pitch test:** ~ (qualified) — plate-01's modern-casual-bistro aesthetic would represent the food menu faithfully but the Gaming Lounge is a risk vector: if we build a clean bistro hero and the owner's photos include slot machines, the pitch breaks. Mitigation: we'd need to structurally IGNORE the gaming lounge in the build (don't feature it, don't photograph it) — some restaurants with video gaming do exactly this on their sites, treating it as a side-room. Plate-01's register is slightly below actual menu tier; varro may be the truer fit.
- **Decision:** BUILD (conditional) — RE-ROUTE to varro-01 OR stay on plate-01 but confirm via menu PDF price check first. Defer one step: pull the actual price PDF before forking. Lead with "SpotHopper-sameness" as the switch case. If menu confirms $$-$$$ ($25+ avg), use varro.
- **Notes:** The gaming lounge is the single biggest risk. Unlike Walnut, reviews don't emphasize it and the food program is legitimately upscale — so it's a "manageable Walnut" not a "fatal Walnut." Decision to build hinges on owner being willing to foreground the restaurant over the gaming, which we can't know pre-contact. Flag for sales convo: "your site should lead with the halibut, not the lounge." Location caveat: this lead is in Lake in the Hills, not Crystal Lake — batch address was wrong in briefing; still McHenry County, still in-region.
