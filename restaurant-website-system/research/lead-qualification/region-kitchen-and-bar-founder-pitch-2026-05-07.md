# Region Kitchen and Bar — Founder Pitch Draft

- Date: 2026-05-07T13:27Z
- Restaurant: Region Kitchen and Bar / Region
- Current site: `https://regionrestaurant.com/`
- Recommended archetype: `roma`
- Use case: founder/demo talking points after MC provisioning and preview build. This is **not** a direct outreach email yet.

## One-sentence thesis

Region already has the food, reputation, service, and special-occasion demand of a polished Barrington dining destination; the website should make that obvious in five seconds instead of opening with operational carryout caveats.

## Why Region is worth pitching

Region is not a low-signal restaurant that needs invented positioning. Public evidence already supports a compelling before/after:

- Chef/owner Dave Perlick gives the restaurant a real story.
- Current menu depth is strong: Field/Stream small plates, serious steak/seafood mains, libations, dessert, and polished sides.
- Google Maps shows `4.8` stars and `699` reviews in the browser evidence packet.
- OpenTable fetch on 2026-05-07 showed `4.9`, `3,010` reviews, `$31 to $50`, Contemporary American, casual elegant, booked `36` times today, and “good for special occasions / neighborhood gem / creative cocktails.”
- Current site already has revenue paths — reservations, Toast ordering, DoorDash/Uber Eats, gift cards, private events, phone, directions — but they are not organized around guest intent.

The pitch is straightforward: Region is already being experienced as upscale, warm, chef-driven, and special-occasion-worthy. The website should sell that experience before it asks guests to parse operations.

## Before/after story

### Current first impression

The current homepage leads with online carryout messaging and warns that to-go ordering may be unavailable when the dining room is busy. That is honest, but it makes the restaurant feel operational before it feels desirable.

Other visible gaps:

- reservation flow feels small and phone-first despite OpenTable visibility in public sources
- private events have strong capacity facts but mostly resolve to an email handoff
- menu content is rich but reads as a long wall of text, especially on mobile
- visual system feels dated against the restaurant quality: orange/dark two-tier header, heavy overlays, empty bands, `© 2018 Region`, GoDaddy badge pattern
- delivery links include safelinks-wrapped/duplicated URLs
- logo alt text mismatch was observed in browser accessibility evidence

### Proposed after state

A Roma-style redesign should open as a chef-driven Barrington dinner destination:

- hero: seasonal American dining, reserve/menu/private events CTAs
- proof: ratings/reviews only where accurately sourced and approved
- menu: real dish names and categories presented editorially
- chef story: Dave Perlick and Region's local culinary point of view
- reservations: OpenTable if active/approved, phone fallback always visible
- private events: capacity, occasions, room/patio/buyout options, direct inquiry
- secondary paths: Toast ordering, verified delivery links, gift cards, directions, hours
- mobile: sticky Reserve / Call / Menu / Directions actions

## Things to avoid in the pitch

- Do not say Region’s current site is “bad” or embarrass the owner.
- Do not imply the restaurant has no online demand; evidence says the opposite.
- Do not overclaim SEO, revenue lift, or booking increases without data.
- Do not quote individual Google reviews verbatim without permission.
- Do not claim direct partnership with Google/OpenTable/Toast.
- Do not promise final provider links until they are rechecked in final QA.
