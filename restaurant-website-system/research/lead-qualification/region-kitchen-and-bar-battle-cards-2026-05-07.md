# Region Kitchen and Bar — Battle Cards

- Date: 2026-05-07T13:27Z
- Restaurant: Region Kitchen and Bar / Region
- Current site: `https://regionrestaurant.com/`
- Recommended archetype: `roma`
- Use case: internal founder/demo prep after preview exists. Do not send raw.

## Core position

Region does not need a generic restaurant website. It needs an editorial, chef/menu-forward site that matches the quality guests already describe: polished, warm, reservation-led, special-occasion friendly, and specific to Barrington.

## Battle card 1 — “We already have a website.”

### Likely owner objection

“We already have a site, and guests can find the menu, phone number, and ordering links.”

### Best response

“That is true — the current site is functional. The opportunity is not basic presence; it is first impression and conversion hierarchy. Right now the homepage leads with carryout limitations and gift-card/order messaging. For a restaurant with Region’s reviews, menu depth, chef story, and private-event capacity, the first impression should sell the dining room, reservation intent, and special-occasion value first.”

### Evidence to reference

- R1 audit shows carryout caveat as the first major homepage message.
- Current site has useful paths but weak hierarchy: reservations, private events, ordering, gift cards, emails.
- Google/OpenTable evidence supports a more premium positioning than the current first screen communicates.

### Demo move

Show current homepage hero beside redesigned hero. Ask: “Which one better matches the experience guests describe after dining here?”

## Battle card 2 — “Most people find us on Google/OpenTable, not our website.”

### Best response

“That makes the official site even more important. Google/OpenTable create intent; the official site should convert and reassure. Guests clicking through should immediately see the same polished experience they saw in reviews: menu depth, reservation confidence, private dining, hours, location, and direct contact.”

### Evidence to reference

- Google Maps browser evidence: `4.8` / `699` reviews.
- OpenTable fetched evidence: `4.9` / `3,010` reviews and booked `36` times today.
- Official site is still the owner-controlled place to package private events, gift cards, menu story, and brand perception.

### Demo move

Open mobile preview and show sticky Reserve / Call / Menu / Directions actions.

## Battle card 3 — “We are phone-first for reservations.”

### Best response

“Phone can stay prominent. The redesign does not remove it. It makes booking intent clearer and gives guests confidence before they call. If OpenTable is active and approved, it can be primary; if phone-first is the actual preference, the site can make that feel intentional instead of underbuilt.”

### Evidence to reference

- Current official Reservations page is phone-first.
- Google Maps surfaced OpenTable.
- OpenTable profile fetch showed an active reservation module.
- R2 brief requires final QA to verify whether OpenTable should be first-class.

### Demo move

Show reservation module with two clear options: Reserve via OpenTable / Call `(224) 848-4005`.

## Battle card 4 — “Private events already have an email listed.”

### Best response

“Listing an email is a contact point, not a sales funnel. Region already has strong private-event facts — groups `15 to 170`, private room `40`, patio `30`, full buyout `170`. The redesign turns those facts into a clear private-events story before asking someone to email Jillian.”

### Evidence to reference

- Current private-events page has concrete capacities.
- Current path ends with `jperlick@regionrestaurant.com`.
- Review evidence supports birthdays, anniversaries, date nights, celebrations, and dinners with friends.

### Demo move

Show private-events section: occasions → capacities → room/patio/buyout → inquiry CTA.

## Battle card 5 — “We do not want to sound too fancy.”

### Best response

“The direction is polished, not pretentious. Roma fits because Region is chef-owned, seasonal, and refined, but still warm and local. The copy should feel like Barrington hospitality — confident, specific, and welcoming.”

### Evidence to reference

- Google reviews describe both upscale/refined atmosphere and warm service.
- OpenTable labels casual elegant, neighborhood gem, good for special occasions.
- R2 brief explicitly bans stiff steakhouse-formal and generic luxury language.

### Demo move

Read the hero line and compare it with a generic “fresh ingredients, great service” line. Region-specific wins.

## Battle card 6 — “Can we use review quotes?”

### Best response

“Not by default. The review packet is excellent for strategy and proof themes, but final public copy should summarize patterns unless the restaurant approves direct quotes. We can say guests consistently praise the food, service, cocktails, atmosphere, and special-occasion experience without copying individual reviewers.”

### Evidence to reference

- Google reviews packet has 30 written reviews and strategy insights.
- Guardrails say no direct quotes/staff names without approval.

### Demo move

Show “review themes” strip rather than quoted testimonials.

## Battle card 7 — “What should be above the fold?”

### Answer

Desktop:

1. Region identity: chef-driven seasonal American dining in Barrington.
2. Strong dining-room/food visual.
3. CTAs: Reserve, View Menu, Private Events.
4. Secondary proof: location, rating/review facts only if QA-approved.

Mobile:

1. Same identity in fewer words.
2. Reserve + Menu CTAs visible immediately.
3. Sticky CTA bar: Reserve, Call, Menu, Directions.

### Why

The first screen should create desire and action. Carryout availability belongs in a secondary ordering module.

## Battle card 8 — Menu strategy

### Owner concern

“Our menu is long and changes; we do not want it to be hard to maintain.”

### Response

“The site should preserve menu fidelity without turning the page into a wall of text. Use categories and a signature-dishes section, then maintain full current items in structured sections. Seasonal change is a feature of the brand, not a problem, as long as the site is easy to update.”

### Must preserve

- Field Small Plates
- Stream Small Plates
- Salad + Soup Plates
- Field Large Plates
- Stream Large Plates
- Escorts / sides
- Dessert
- Libations

### High-proof dishes to verify before featuring

Bang Bang Cauliflower, sticky toffee pudding, Chateaubriand crostini, ricotta, short ribs, duck confit, filet meatloaf, burgers, San Fran Cioppino, walleye, ribeye/NY strip, lobster tail, sashimi tuna tartare, corn crème brûlée, salmon, cocktails.

## Battle card 9 — Visual QA risks

### Risks to watch

- Design drifts into generic steakhouse/luxury template.
- Too much dark overlay makes the food feel heavy.
- Menu still looks like a text dump.
- Mobile loses reservation/private-event CTAs.
- Stock imagery replaces real Region identity.
- Review claims become too direct or unsupported.
- Delivery links remain safelinks-wrapped or duplicated.
- Old alt text/metadata mismatch survives.

### QA response

Reject the preview until Region feels specifically chef-owned, seasonal, Barrington-based, and conversion-ready on mobile.

## Battle card 10 — Demo path for Ethan

1. Start with the current homepage screenshot: carryout-first, dated visual system.
2. Show the new hero: reservation/menu/private-events first.
3. Scroll to signature menu proof: real dish names, not generic copy.
4. Show chef story: Dave Perlick and seasonal American framing.
5. Show reservation module: OpenTable/phone fallback, hours, location.
6. Show private events: capacities and occasions.
7. Show mobile sticky CTA bar.
8. Close with factual QA: no fake claims, real links, current menu/hours verified.

## Battle card 11 — If owner asks “what changes before launch?”

Answer:

- confirm OpenTable vs phone-first preference
- verify DoorDash/Uber Eats clean links or omit them
- confirm public emails and private-event contact
- provide/approve preferred photos if available
- verify current seasonal menu items
- approve whether review quotes/staff names can be used; default is no

## Battle card 12 — Red lines

Do not ship if any of these are true:

- homepage still leads with carryout limitations
- no clear reservation path above fold
- mobile has no sticky high-intent CTA
- menu is still a tiny dense wall
- private events are just an email line
- fake awards/testimonials/review quotes appear
- provider links are placeholders or broken
- alt text references another restaurant
- design could be mistaken for any generic “upscale restaurant”

## Final internal recommendation

Region should be pitched only after the preview proves the before/after visually. The evidence is strong enough: the restaurant already has demand and reputation. The sale should not be framed as “you need a website”; it should be framed as “your official first impression should match the dining experience people already love.”
