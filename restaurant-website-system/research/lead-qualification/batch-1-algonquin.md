### #1 Montarra — Algonquin, IL
- **Visual fit:** ✓ — dark-wood dining room, Chihuly-inspired chandeliers, water wall; reviewers use "elegance of downtown Chicago," "classy," "sophisticated ambiance." Matches 1776's dark warm-upscale destination register.
- **Menu register:** $$-$$$ (avg entrée ~$32 from sample: Filet Mignon $36, Skirt Steak $31, Beef+Scallop Duet $29, plus rack of lamb / fresh fish / bouillabaisse implied $30-45; cheapest $29, priciest ~$45+). Sits at the low end of $$$ — closer to "upscale destination" than true fine-dining; matches 1776 tier but NOT alinea/qitchen.
- **Review tone:** "top-notch," "hidden gem," "elegant," "Chicago-caliber suburban." 4.4★ Yelp (633 reviews), 2414 OpenTable reviews. Clearly upscale-destination tone.
- **Current site:** 7/10 — Squarespace, live (earlier ECONNREFUSED was transient), modern typography, OpenTable widget above the fold, clean hero of dining room, mobile nav working. No obvious dated cues.
- **Switch reason:** VAGUE — site is actually functioning well with OpenTable integration; nothing concrete beyond "could be more cinematic." No broken fundamentals.
- **Switching cost:** medium-high — OpenTable integrated, Squarespace build, 20+ yrs operating history, likely email list / events programming.
- **Pitch test:** ✓ — 1776's dark-warm-upscale aesthetic WOULD represent Montarra faithfully. Register is right.
- **Decision:** SKIP (doing fine — 7+/10 site + no concrete failure). Re-evaluate if they replatform or a specific pain point surfaces.
- **Notes:** Exactly the kind of lead where pitch would land on register but fail on "why switch." Save as "if we get introduced" not "cold outbound."
