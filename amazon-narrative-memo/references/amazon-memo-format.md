# amazon memo format

## what this is
A short reference on Amazon-style narrative documents and how to adapt them for founder/operator work.

## why the format works
The point is not the page count. The point is forced clarity.

Narrative memos work because they:
- force the author to think through the argument before the meeting
- give every reader the same starting context
- slow down hand-wavy presentation theater
- make tradeoffs and assumptions easier to inspect
- separate real thinking from charisma

This lines up with public descriptions of Amazon's writing culture: deliberate reading, shared context, and narrative docs used for discovery, review, and decision-making.

## 2-pager vs 6-pager vs prfaq
### 2-pager
Use when:
- the scope is narrow
- the decision is mostly binary or bounded
- the audience needs a fast pre-read
- you are framing a recommendation, not replacing a full strategy doc

Suggested shape:
1. decision / recommendation
2. why now
3. evidence
4. key tradeoff
5. next step

### 6-7 pager
Use when:
- the problem has multiple channels, constraints, or stakeholder tradeoffs
- the reader needs enough context to judge a strategy, not just a tactic
- the deliverable should stand on its own without live narration

Suggested shape:
1. recommendation
2. context / market truth
3. evidence and examples
4. options considered
5. recommended plan
6. implementation path
7. risks, unknowns, approvals needed

### prfaq
Use when:
- you are pitching a new product or launch motion
- customer value should be clarified before build
- the team needs to pressure-test demand, differentiation, and objections

## recommendation for binky
For Binky launch strategy, use a **6-7 page narrative memo** as the main artifact.

Why:
- the question is not one narrow decision
- it spans acquisition channels, messaging, creator strategy, organic content, controversy/risk boundaries, and launch sequencing
- Ethan wants a durable operating memo, not a thin pre-read

Use a **2-pager** only as a follow-on summary or exec brief after the long memo exists.

## practical drafting checklist
- can the first paragraph stand alone as the answer?
- does each major claim have evidence, a real example, or direct experience behind it?
- are the rejected options real and fairly represented?
- does the memo end with concrete next actions?
- could someone read this cold and know what to do next?

## light source notes
- The PRFAQ writing-culture article describes Amazon's narrative-doc culture as a mechanism for discovery, review, and decision through deliberate reading.
- Broader public summaries of the Amazon 6-pager consistently frame the memo as a structured narrative replacing slide-led discussion.

For this skill, those ideas matter more than any one canonical public template.
