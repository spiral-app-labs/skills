# Amazon Narrative Memo

Use this skill when a doc should function like a real narrative memo instead of a deck in paragraph clothing.

## Core rule
Write the memo so a smart reader can understand the argument silently before discussion starts. The doc should carry the thinking, not depend on live explanation.

## Pick the right format
- **2-pager**: use for a fast decision pre-read, framing note, or narrow recommendation. Good for one decision, one tradeoff, one next step.
- **6-7 pager**: use for strategy, launch plans, major bets, or multi-variable recommendations. This is the default for important operator/founder decisions.
- **PRFAQ**: use when pitching a new product, launch concept, or customer-facing initiative that benefits from working backward from the press release and FAQ.

## Recommended structure for a 6-7 pager
1. **opening claim**
   - one paragraph stating the recommendation and why it matters now.
2. **problem / opportunity**
   - what is true in the market or product today.
3. **evidence**
   - research, examples, prior experiments, internal observations.
4. **options**
   - realistic paths, not strawmen.
5. **recommendation**
   - what to do, what not to do, and why this path wins.
6. **execution plan**
   - phases, owners, constraints, immediate next steps.
7. **risks and open questions**
   - what could fail, what needs validation, what needs founder approval.

## Writing rules
- lead with the recommendation early.
- use full sentences and paragraphs, not slide bullets.
- every major claim needs evidence, an example, or a clear argument.
- name tradeoffs explicitly.
- prefer plain language over consultant fluff.
- if a section only sounds smart but changes no decision, cut it.

## Binky-specific guidance
For Binky marketing or launch strategy:
- default to a **6-7 page narrative memo**, not a 2-pager.
- include what is working now, why it works, where controversy or tension helps, and where brand risk starts.
- tie channels and tactics back to user psychology, economics, and execution feasibility.
- end with a concrete recommendation stack: do now, test next, avoid for now.
