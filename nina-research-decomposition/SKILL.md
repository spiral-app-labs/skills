# nina-research-decomposition

Use this skill when Nina gets a fuzzy or oversized research ask.

## Trigger Conditions
- "what is world class influencer marketing?"
- "research this market"
- "figure out what's working"
- any prompt that is broad, strategic, and underspecified

## Goal
Turn a vague ask into a research plan that can actually be executed.

## Workflow
1. **Find the real decision**
   - What choice is the founder trying to make?
   - What would change if the answer were different?

2. **Classify the ask**
   Common buckets:
   - strategy benchmark
   - channel analysis
   - competitor / market map
   - creator ecosystem scan
   - copy / positioning research
   - execution playbook extraction

3. **Break it into 4-7 sub-questions**
   Example pattern:
   - what do the best operators actually do?
   - what outcomes suggest it works?
   - what are the common mechanisms/patterns?
   - what breaks or fails in practice?
   - what would transfer to our context?

4. **Define evidence standards**
   Prefer evidence like:
   - repeated patterns across multiple winners
   - concrete examples, numbers, screenshots, or behaviors
   - recency
   - counterexamples that disprove lazy conclusions

5. **Choose source buckets**
   Pull from a mix of:
   - direct competitors
   - adjacent winners
   - practitioners/operators
   - user behavior or distribution evidence
   - platform-native examples

6. **Name the output format before researching**
   Usually one of:
   - recommendation memo
   - ranked options list
   - pattern map
   - insight list + next-step tasks
   - research-backed copy brief

## Output Contract
Before deep research, Nina should be able to state:
- the decision being answered
- the narrowed research scope
- the key sub-questions
- what evidence would count
- the shape of the final deliverable

## Quality Bar
- do not keep the original vague question untouched
- do not research everything equally
- do not confuse topic breadth with useful scope
- optimize for decision quality, not topic coverage

## Escalate If
- two different interpretations of the ask would lead to materially different work
- the ask mixes research, product, and execution in a way that needs founder prioritization
- the expected output is unclear after one decomposition pass
